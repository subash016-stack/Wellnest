Protection from Domestic Violence

Definition

Domestic violence refers to any form of abuse—physical, emotional, psychological, or financial—within a household or intimate relationship. It is a serious violation of human rights.

Legal Protection

Many countries have specific laws and acts that protect individuals from domestic violence, offering immediate protection orders, restraining orders, and legal recourse against abusers.

Support Services

Victims can access helplines, legal aid, counseling, and shelter homes. Many organizations and government bodies offer 24/7 support for those in crisis.

Reporting Abuse

Abuse can be reported to local authorities, women's help centers, or through emergency helplines. Confidentiality and safety measures are often prioritized.

Your Rights

Everyone has the right to live free from violence and fear. Victims have the right to safety, justice, protection, and access to rehabilitation and support services.
