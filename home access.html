Shelter Home Access

What Are Shelter Homes?

Shelter homes are safe spaces provided by the government or NGOs for individuals—especially women and children—facing violence, abuse, or homelessness. These facilities offer temporary accommodation, protection, and support services like legal aid and counseling.

Legal Rights & Government Support

  • Under laws like the Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005, victims have the right to safe shelter.
  • One Stop Centers (OSCs) and Swadhar Greh schemes support women in distress with shelter and legal aid.
  • You can request protection and shelter through local authorities or national helplines.

How to Access Shelter Homes

  • Call Women’s Helpline 181 or the national emergency number 112.
  • Visit your nearest police station or District Protection Officer (DPO).
  • Contact verified NGOs that operate shelter homes and crisis centers.
  • Approach the Legal Services Authority (LSA) office in your district.

Additional Services Available

  • Legal support and assistance in filing complaints.
  • Medical care and trauma counseling.
  • Vocational training and rehabilitation programs.
