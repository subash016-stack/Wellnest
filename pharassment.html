Protection Against Harassment

What is Harassment?

Harassment is any unwelcome behavior—verbal, physical, emotional, or sexual—that offends, humiliates, or intimidates another person. It can happen at work, school, in public spaces, or online.

Examples include: inappropriate comments, touching without consent, stalking, cyberbullying, and threats.

Types of Harassment

  • Sexual Harassment: Unwanted sexual advances or comments.
  • Workplace Harassment: Discrimination, bullying, or power abuse at work.
  • Cyber Harassment: Online threats, abusive messages, or non-consensual sharing of images.
  • Street Harassment: Catcalling, stalking, or physical intimidation in public spaces.

Your Rights

  • Right to a safe workplace under laws like the POSH Act (India).
  • Right to report harassment to authorities or internal committees.
  • Right to confidentiality and protection during investigations.

What To Do If You're Harassed

  1. Stay Safe: Remove yourself from danger if possible.
  2. Document Everything: Keep records of messages, dates, or witness names.
  3. Report It: File a complaint with HR, Internal Committees, or the police.
  4. Seek Support: Talk to a trusted person or counselor.
