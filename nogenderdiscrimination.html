No Gender Discrimination

Eligibility

All women employees working in government or private organizations are protected under anti-discrimination laws. This applies regardless of experience, designation, or contract type.

Legal Protections

Indian laws guarantee that women have the right to a workplace free from gender-based bias. Employers must ensure fairness in hiring, training, promotions, salary, and job responsibilities.

Important Acts

Equal Remuneration Act, 1976 ensures equal pay for equal work.

Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace Act, 2013 provides protection and redressal.

Articles 15 & 16 of the Indian Constitution prohibit sex-based discrimination and ensure equal employment opportunities.

Examples of Discrimination

Examples include unequal pay, exclusion in meetings, denial of promotions, or being asked marital plans during interviews.

Compensation

Employers may face legal actions like back wages, reinstatement, emotional distress compensation, or government penalties.
