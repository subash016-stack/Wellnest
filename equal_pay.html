Equal Pay for Equal Work

Overview

Equal pay for equal work ensures that employees receive the same compensation for performing the same or equivalent job duties, regardless of gender, race, or other protected characteristics.

Legal Protections

Various labor laws and regulations prohibit wage discrimination and support pay equity. These may include national equal pay acts, labor codes, and human rights legislation.

Identifying Pay Gaps

Employees can request transparency in pay structures and conduct comparisons within their organization. Disparities in pay for the same roles may be grounds for legal action.

Employee Rights

Employees have the right to file complaints if they experience unequal pay. Employers are prohibited from retaliating against individuals who raise pay equity concerns.

Employer Responsibilities

Employers must ensure fair compensation practices, conduct regular pay audits, and eliminate unjustified wage disparities to maintain compliance and promote workplace equality.
