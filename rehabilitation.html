Rehabilitation and Care for Affected Children

Overview

Children who have faced abuse, exploitation, neglect, or conflict require specialized care to heal, recover, and reintegrate into society. Rehabilitation ensures their emotional, physical, and social well-being.

Key Services Provided

  • Short- and long-term shelter in child care institutions.
  • Counseling and psychological support.
  • Access to healthcare, nutrition, and education.
  • Skill development and vocational training for older children.

Role of Authorities

Child Welfare Committees and District Child Protection Units coordinate with NGOs, medical professionals, and schools to ensure that each child receives an individualized care plan.
