Protection from Child Labour

What is Child Labour?

Child labour refers to work that deprives children of their childhood, education, and development. It is often exploitative, harmful, and against the law.

Legal Safeguards

Laws prohibit employment of children below a certain age and protect them from hazardous and exploitative work. Employers violating these laws face strict penalties.

Right to Education

Children must be in schools, not workplaces. Free and compulsory education helps prevent child labour and empowers children for a better future.

Support and Rehabilitation

Rescued children are entitled to counseling, vocational training, and reintegration programs to help them return to school or acquire safe skills.
