Right to Legal Aid and Representation

Overview

Children have the right to access legal support and fair representation in any legal matters that concern them, including cases of abuse, neglect, custody, or conflict with the law.

Importance of Legal Aid

  • Ensures children's voices are heard in court or legal settings.
  • Protects their rights during investigation and trial processes.
  • Helps navigate complex legal procedures with proper guidance.

Government Responsibility

The state is obligated to provide free legal services for children who cannot afford them, especially in cases involving vulnerable or marginalized groups.

Where to Seek Help

Legal aid cells, child welfare committees, and registered NGOs offer assistance to children in need of legal representation. Child helplines can also guide families to the appropriate resources.
